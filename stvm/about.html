About Late Night Scholarships

Each year, a new graduating class from Perry has the opportunity to leave PHS and further their education by attending an accredited university. However, this opportunity is threatened as the sticker price on collegiate programs continues to rise and federal support is reduced. Fortunately, each year a new class of Perry alumni graduates from universities across the nation and enters the workforce. To support current high school students who have the academic prowess to attend college but lack the financial resources to do so, we have created the Late Night Scholarships fund.

Registered with the Ohio Secretary of State and the IRS as a 501(c)(3) exempt non-profit, Late Night Scholarships was created to organize Perry alumni who want to directly support Perry students seeking financial assistance to attend college.

While Late Night Scholarships is officially a non-profit organization, it functions more as a community of alumni philanthropists supporting young intellectuals. Each of the donors involved in the fund collaborates to manage the scholarship process. By donating as little as $5, donors have the option to read the anonymized applications and vote for which applicants should receive the scholarships.

Furthermore, each donor is welcome to propose improvements to the applications. In the future, we hope to provide mentorship opportunities to applicants by matching students with donors based on their career aspirations. In 2020, we awarded scholarships of $500 to 3 graduating PHS seniors. The hope is that as more alumni graduate from college, the donor base will continue to grow and help us provide more scholarships with larger balances.

For anyone interested in becoming more involved, please reach out using the email or phone number provided in the "Contact" page.
